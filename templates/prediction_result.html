Performance Metrics Explained

Accuracy: The ratio of correctly predicted instances to the total instances. It is calculated as:

Accuracy = (TP + TN) / (TP + TN + FP + FN)

where TP is True Positives, TN is True Negatives, FP is False Positives, and FN is False Negatives.

ROC AUC Score: The Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic Curve. This metric evaluates the ability of the model to discriminate between positive and negative classes. A higher score indicates better performance.

ROC AUC is calculated by plotting the True Positive Rate (TPR) against the False Positive Rate (FPR) at various threshold settings and calculating the area under this curve.

F1 Score: The harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a balance between the two. It is especially useful for imbalanced datasets. It is calculated as:

F1 Score = 2 * (Precision * Recall) / (Precision + Recall)

where Precision = TP / (TP + FP) and Recall = TP / (TP + FN).
